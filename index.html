About Me

I am Balram, a passionate postgraduate student currently pursuing my Master of Technology (M.Tech) at the prestigious Indian Institute of Technology, Delhi (IIT Delhi). My academic journey is fueled by an insatiable curiosity for science and the desire to contribute to meaningful technological innovations.

My core research area lies in the study of the magnetic properties of nanoparticles, a subfield of nanotechnology that explores how materials behave at the atomic and molecular level. Unlike bulk materials, nanoparticles exhibit unique quantum and surface effects that dramatically influence their magnetic behavior.

My current work involves synthesizing and characterizing magnetic nanoparticles with the goal of improving their stability, responsiveness, and applicability in advanced technologies. These materials have vast potential across biomedical applications such as MRI contrast agents and drug delivery systems, as well as in energy storage and environmental sensing.

I use tools like XRD, VSM, TEM, and SQUID to analyze particle morphology, magnetic hysteresis, coercivity, and saturation magnetization. Understanding these characteristics helps in tailoring materials for specific applications.

Beyond the lab, I am fascinated by interdisciplinary research that blends physics, chemistry, and engineering. I strive to push the boundaries of what’s possible through focused experimentation, collaboration, and continuous learning.
